Are Muslim community festivals and suhoor fests permissible in Islam?

Not categorically haram. Declaring a large Muslim public gathering haram simply because some may act improperly is an overreach. Whether attending is advisable depends on your level of piety and intention. For the highly religious, spending Ramadan in worship is better. For those at lower levels of practice, it may actually serve as an iman boost. Actions are judged by intentions.

Is it haram to talk to the opposite gender in Islam? What are the limits of male-female interaction?

Talking to the opposite gender is not haram in itself — no scholar has ever said so. What IS explicitly haram by hadith is khalwa (private seclusion alone with a non-mahram). The principle of sadd al-dhara'i is valid but culturally sensitive. Context, intention, and content all matter.

Is it permissible for Muslims to celebrate religious holidays of other faiths like Christmas or Diwali?

Muslims do not celebrate religious festivals of other faiths. However, Muslim converts may attend family gatherings on Christmas to maintain family ties, as long as they avoid religious rituals.

Are personal private celebrations like birthdays, graduations, and promotions permissible in Islam?

Personal, private, non-religious celebrations are fully permissible. The Sharia is silent about them, and making something annual (like birthdays) does not make it bid'ah.

How should a Muslim community handle differences of opinion and practices within a single Masjid?

When the Muslim world's mainstream scholars have disagreed about a practice, a small local community cannot enforce one view on the other. Live and let live: allow both sides to state their positions respectfully, permit the practices that have scholarly precedent, and never split the community over issues that remain contested across the Muslim world.

How should a Muslim respond to the LGBTQ+ movement in a respectful but principled way?

Islam clearly prohibits same-sex relationships and gender transition. However, Muslims must communicate this with prophetic wisdom — not by acquiescing under social pressure, nor by speaking with vulgarity. Same-sex attraction itself is not a sin; acting on it is. Muslims can and should remain kind and civil with LGBTQ individuals in daily life.

Why do Muslims not celebrate Pride Month? What is the Islamic position on same-sex relationships?

Same-sex actions are haram by unanimous 14-century scholarly consensus. The Quran's story of Lut addresses this topic explicitly and repeatedly. Muslims cannot celebrate Pride Month but must distinguish between hating the act and hating the person — cooperation for generic good is permitted, but facilitating or normalizing what the Sharia prohibits is not.

Is sarcasm halal or haram in Islam?

Sarcasm among friends where everyone is laughing together and no one is targeted or hurt is permissible. Sarcasm meant to denigrate, belittle, or hurt someone's feelings is not permissible. The key distinction: laughing with people is allowed; laughing at people is not.

Does the Quran forbid Muslims from having non-Muslim friends?

The Quran does not forbid Muslims from having non-Muslim friends. The verse about 'awliya' refers to community-level political/military alliances during conflict, not personal friendships. Allah explicitly permits the highest loyalty (birr) toward non-Muslims who are not hostile.
