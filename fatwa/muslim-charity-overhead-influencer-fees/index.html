Is it ethical for Muslim charities to pay high overhead costs and influencer fees? What percentage is acceptable?

Yasir Qadhi • March 16, 2026 • Watch on YouTube
Muslim charity overheadIslamic charity ethicsinfluencer charity feescharity percentage Islamis charity overhead halal

Quick Answer

Some charity overhead is inevitable and Islamically legitimate — the Quran itself allocates one of the eight categories of zakat to those who collect zakat (amils). Most reputable Islamic charities have 15–20% overhead, which Yasir Qadhi considers good. Overhead of 50–60% is unacceptable and he refuses to work with such organizations. He personally considers percentage-based fees for influencers or fundraisers to be absolutely unethical — his own condition is always a flat fee, and his honorarium comes from the organizers' funds, never from the donations raised. However, the question of whether it is better to raise zero vs. raising millions with some going to unethical intermediaries is genuinely complex and does not have a simple answer.
