Is it permissible to drink water while standing?

Yasir Qadhi • April 22, 2025 • Watch on YouTube
drinkingstandingadabetiquettesunnah

Quick Answer

It is better (mustahabb) to sit down when drinking water if you are able to, but it is not prohibited to drink while standing. Ibn Abbas drank water while standing and said he saw the Prophet do the same. When circumstances make standing natural (water fountain, office water cooler), there is no issue. Sitting is preferred when you are at home and have the luxury to do so.

Full Lecture Transcript (Cleaned)

The Ruling — 23:02

No doubt it is better, when you're able to, to sit down when drinking. However, there are a number of traditions showing permissibility of standing.

For example, Ibn Abbas drank water while standing, and then he looked at somebody staring at him and said: "I saw the Prophet (peace be upon him) drink water while standing" -- as if to say, "Don't look at me like that, I saw the Prophet do it."

The Scholarly Position — 23:35

What the scholars say is that when the circumstances warrant it -- like at a drinking fountain (what are you going to do?), or at your office water cooler where everybody is standing -- no problem.

But if you are at home and you have the luxury, then it is better out of adab to sit. However, it is not something that is mandated.
