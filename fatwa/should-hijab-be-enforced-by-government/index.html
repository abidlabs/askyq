Should the hijab be enforced by an Islamic government?

Yasir Qadhi • September 28, 2022 • Watch on YouTube
hijabhijab enforcementIslamic governmentmorality policewomen in Islam

Quick Answer

Yasir Qadhi affirms that hijab is a clear religious obligation in Islam according to scholarly consensus, but argues that enforcing it through government coercion is counterproductive and not aligned with the spirit of the Sharia. He emphasizes that Islam's political theory differs fundamentally from Western secularism, that no government is truly morally neutral, and that historically Islamic civilizations did not use morality police to enforce dress codes. He advises focusing on personal piety rather than debating enforcement in distant lands, and advocates for positive encouragement over punitive enforcement.
