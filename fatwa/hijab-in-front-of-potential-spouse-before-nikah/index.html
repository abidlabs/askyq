May a woman uncover her hair for a man who is proposing to her before the nikah?

Yasir Qadhi • October 20, 2020 • Watch on YouTube

Quick Answer

Hanafi, Maliki, and Shafi'i generally require full hijab before nikah even in supervised meetings. The Hanbali school (with evidence such as the hadith encouraging looking at a prospective spouse) allows her to appear before the suitor as she would before her mahrams (e.g., hair uncovered in modest clothing) if she chooses—not forced. Meetings should always be with mahrams present.
