Is it permissible for Muslims to celebrate religious holidays of other faiths like Christmas or Diwali?

Muslims do not celebrate religious festivals of other faiths. However, Muslim converts may attend family gatherings on Christmas to maintain family ties, as long as they avoid religious rituals.

Is it permissible for Muslims to celebrate Halloween?

Halloween should be avoided due to lingering associations with demons and the supernatural, but it does not constitute shirk or kufr. Muslims who participate out of ignorance should be treated with empathy.

What constitutes impermissible imitation of non-Muslims (tashabbuh)?

Prohibited imitation means deliberately adopting something that characterizes a civilization of kufr foreign to one's own. For Western Muslims, speaking English and wearing Western clothes is not imitation.

Are personal private celebrations like birthdays, graduations, and promotions permissible in Islam?

Personal, private, non-religious celebrations are fully permissible. The Sharia is silent about them, and making something annual (like birthdays) does not make it bid'ah.
