How should a Muslim husband balance between his mother and his wife?

Yasir Qadhi • September 6, 2025 • Watch on YouTube

Quick Answer

A Muslim husband must simultaneously be a dutiful son and a protector of his wife — he cannot take sides. The Quran commands ihsan (kindness) to parents, not unconditional obedience, which is a critical distinction. Parents do not have the right to interfere in the personal details of their son's marriage. If a mother says something about the wife, the husband absorbs it himself and does not relay it. The husband should minimize direct hostility between the two women, set boundaries, and understand that this tension is a universal, natural part of life across all cultures.
