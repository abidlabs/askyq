Is it heresy to question the hadith about Aisha's age at marriage? Can Muslims reject the narration saying she was 9?

Quick Answer

Questioning the narration that Aisha (RA) was 9 at the time of consummation is NOT heresy and NOT a point of theology — it is a point of history. Those who accept the narration have valid arguments; those who question it also have valid scholarly arguments. Both positions are within mainstream Islam. The hadith comes from Hisham ibn Urwah, who never actually met Aisha (he was her grand-nephew), and some scholars of hadith have pointed out a mathematical discrepancy. Yasir Qadhi declines to say which position is correct, but explicitly states that following the opinion that she was older involves no heresy.

Do understand that rejecting a specific hadith should not be done without scholarly precedent. For you, if this is a very big issue and you think the other opinion makes sense — I am telling you, go ahead and take it. No big deal.
