Is a 401K early withdrawal repayment penalty considered haram interest?

Yasir Qadhi • October 19, 2021 • Watch on YouTube
401k repayment penalty haram401k interest islamis 401k penalty ribaretirement account withdrawal islam401k loan penalty halal

Quick Answer

The 401K repayment penalty is **not interest (riba)**. Interest involves a payment from one party to another third party. In this case, the penalty goes from your own pocket back into your own account — there is no third party. **Not every financial penalty is riba.** When the penalty is from you, upon you, and returned to you, there is no interest involved.

Brother Tadek from New Jersey emails. He has a 401K retirement account and needs to withdraw money from it for a personal reason. However, his company requires that when he repays the withdrawal, he must put in extra money as a penalty. He asks: is this extra amount considered interest? Is it halal for him to do this?

Yasir Qadhi's response is brief and direct: it is not interest at all, because the money is going from your pocket back into your own pocket. There is no third party involved. Not every penalty is interest. When the penalty is from you, upon you, and returned to you — and no outside party receives anything — there is no riba. And that is that.
