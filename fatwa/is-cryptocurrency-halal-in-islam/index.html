Is cryptocurrency halal in Islam?

Yasir Qadhi • August 23, 2019 • Watch on YouTube
cryptocurrencybitcoincrypto tradinghalal investingis bitcoin halal

Quick Answer

Yasir Qadhi states that cryptocurrency, especially major established coins like Bitcoin, is permissible in principle because no definitive Shariah prohibition applies. He adds that currency-exchange rules still apply, so crypto trades should be spot/instant and not delayed. He also advises caution and discourages risky speculation with money one cannot afford to lose.

Core Ruling on Cryptocurrency — 0:26

The question asks for the Islamic ruling on cryptocurrencies. Yasir Qadhi explains that crypto is a decentralized form of digital commodity/currency and notes that Bitcoin became the most prominent example. He mentions that a fiqh council in North America studied the issue in depth and reached a consensus that no objection rose to the level of making cryptocurrency categorically haram.

He reiterates a foundational legal maxim in transactions: things are permissible unless proven otherwise by clear Shariah evidence. Applying this to crypto, he says the base ruling remains permissibility for major established cryptocurrencies, especially Bitcoin.

Conditions and Cautions — 5:04

He adds that because crypto can function like currency exchange, spot-trade requirements apply: exchanges should be immediate rather than delayed. He also distinguishes between fiqh permissibility and investment advice, warning listeners not to behave foolishly with wealth and not to invest amounts they cannot afford to lose.

He concludes that while crypto is generally permissible in his analysis, each specific token may still require case-by-case review, and Muslims should combine permissibility with prudence.
